Welcome to the Andean School on Cosmology

The Andean School on Cosmology wants to bring together students from the Andean region with world leading experts in the area of cosmology in its theoretical, numerical and observational aspects.

Format

The School will run for 4 weeks from June 1st through June 26th, 2015. Each week will have a different focus. Each day of the The School will have daily lectures (1.5 hours), hands-on sessions (2.5 hours) plus free-format work in the Colaboratorio (a computing lab) at Uniandes. The course will have a total of 64 hours.

Due to space limitations in the computing labs we have a limit of 25 students in the hands-on sessions. However, the lectures are open to a larger attendance.

The instruction languages will be English and Spanish.

Lecturers

  • Miguel Aragon-Calvo (Riverside)
  • Florian Beutler (Berkeley)
  • Jaime E. Forero-Romero (Bogota)
  • Stefan Gottloeber (Potsdam)
  • Yehuda Hoffman (Jerusalem)

Program

  • First Week (Hoffman) Basic analytical aspects of the dynamics and statistics of structure formation in an expanding Universe.

  • Second Week (Gottloeber/Forero-Romero) Simulating structure formation in a dark matter dominated Universe.

  • Third Week (Beutler) Observations of large scale structure and cosmological parameter estimation through BAO measurements.

  • Fourth Week (Aragon-Calvo/Forero-Romero) Finding and visualizing the cosmic web.

Financial Support

We can provide financial support (housing and food) for students not living in Bogota. Priority will be given to students from Bolivia, Chile, Ecuador, Peru and Venezuela that will stay the 4 weeks in Bogota.
